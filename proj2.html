The Project

For this project, I was tasked with redesigning a website for an audio-visual company whose previous site was outdated and hadn’t been updated since its initial creation. The old design lacked a logical flow, making it difficult for customers to navigate easily. My goal was to create a new design that felt intuitive and user-friendly, guiding customers smoothly through the site. The redesign focused on creating a clear path to important actions, such as booking an appointment, ensuring that users could find what they needed quickly and effortlessly.

Role: Lead Designer

Tools: Figma

2024

The Problem

The problem was what the site looked like in the beginning. It
was very unorganized and it looked very outdated. There was not
a straightforward process to see what they offer or to
make an appointment. Because the site looked very outdated, it
could lead to people not making appointments, and therefore,
leading to decrease in business.

The Solution

My idea was to get the site a new-look so that it looks modern
and more organized. The most important part of the design is
to make it straightforward to see what they offer and to
make an appointment for the product of the customers. All
the changes would help the business to grow as you would not
have to rely on people calling to make an appointment, but
they could also book it online.

Target User

Victoria Sinclair, is a Grandmother that needs a TV installed because enjoys to watch movies with her grandchildren when they come to visit. 
Pain Point: She doesn't know how to set up a tv by herself
Goal: Help victoria set up an appointment for someone to help her install the TV.

Alexander, is a tech company owner that wants a elaborate sound system in his backyard to listen to his music
Pain Point: He doesn’t have the time to set it up himself
Goal: Allow for Alexander to book an appointment for someone to come and install the audio system while he is at work.

Brainstorming

Before embarking on the design of the new website version, I aimed to understand what users perceived as strengths and weaknesses of the old site. After conducting several interviews, common issues emerged. One prominent issue was the difficulty in booking appointments, as it lacked clarity and straightforwardness. There were no clear actions leading to appointment bookings, with nearly all interviewees describing the site as disorganized. Another notable issue was its outdated design compared to modern standards. Furthermore, before proceeding with the design process, I conducted a final interview with the site stakeholders to better grasp their desired aesthetics and functionality. Utilizing insights from these interviews, I compiled a list of essential features and functions for inclusion in the redesign. Once the necessary design elements were identified, the subsequent step involved defining the target audience and user demographics.

Wireframes

As I finished up the paper wireframes I started to move them over into
figma to created a more detailed digital wireframe. As the digital
wireframes took shape, I changed up the lay out of the site quite a bit
by adding more packages to choose from, and I made the review section
smaller to make it less cluttered as well as free up space. I was also
able to get an overall feel for the site as more and more pages were
completed
After I created the first set of wireframes, I made them on to a lo-fi
prototype to get some testing down and see how the design held up with
users using the site.
While testing the design it was apparent that the home page needed to
be changed as it did not perform as intended, and it was very awkward
to look at as a user. I decided to change it to be more of an
introduction page to the company to gain the users trust, so when it
came to booking an appointment, they felt like this is a trusted company.

Testing

The testing phase for the redesigned website was overall successful, with the site largely meeting its usability and design objectives. The primary goals were to ensure intuitive navigation, seamless functionality, and alignment with the stakeholders’ vision. Testing involved several rounds of user testing and stakeholder feedback, allowing us to identify and address minor areas for improvement. Overall, the testing process was a success. User and stakeholder feedback helped us refine the website to ensure it provided an intuitive, user-friendly experience that aligned closely with the brand’s goals. The final website is now ready to offer customers an efficient path to discovering, browsing, and booking services with confidence.

Final Product

The final product of the website redesign has proven successful based on comprehensive testing. Users navigated the site effortlessly and encountered no difficulties in booking appointments, and it matched the needs of the stakeholders intentions. With the imminent launch, the next step is to compare its performance against the old site. To thoroughly assess the effectiveness of the redesign, one proposed method is to analyze the proportion of appointments booked through the website compared to those made via phone. By tracking these metrics over time following the launch, we can see the impact of the redesign. If there's a notable increase in appointments booked through the website compared to previous methods, it would indicate a successful outcome of the redesign process.

Teachings From the Project

This project marked a significant success for me as it provided the opportunity to collaborate with a real client with distinct needs. The final product not only met but exceeded expectations in terms of both aesthetics and functionality. Nevertheless, upon reflection, I recognize areas for improvement in future projects. Specifically, I aim to allocate more time to the initial design phase to get a deeper understanding of user preferences and requirements from the outset. Additionally, I intend to engage stakeholders earlier in the process to ensure alignment with their expectations and project direction. Overall, this project served as a valuable learning experience, presenting novel challenges and lessons that I will carry forward into future projects.
