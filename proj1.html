SpecSelect

The easy way to shop electronics

The Project

SpecSelect is a mobile app designed to help users effortlessly compare prices and performance for tech products like laptops, smartphones, and cameras. The app’s intuitive flow guides users through browsing, side-by-side comparisons, and filtering by brand, budget, and features. With built-in price tracking, deal alerts, and wishlist options, SpecSelect makes decision-making easy and efficient. Expert reviews and user ratings offer balanced insights, empowering users to make confident choices. The clean, minimal design prioritizes clarity, ensuring users can find, compare, and select the best products with ease. SpecSelect streamlines the tech shopping experience for < value-focused, informed buyers.

Role: Lead Designer

Tools: Figma

2023

The Problem

Shopping for electronics, especially items like laptops, can be a daunting task. Consumers often face the challenge of sifting through numerous websites to compare prices, specifications, and reviews, which quickly becomes time-consuming and overwhelming. Each site may present information differently, requiring users to jump back and forth to make sense of the options and features available. This scattered approach makes it difficult to identify the best product for their needs and increases the risk of missing out on the best deals or overlooking important details. As a result, the process often feels confusing and exhausting, leaving consumers uncertain about their choices.

The Solution

The app's idea revolves around simplifying the complex process of shopping for electronics, specifically laptops. It offers a centralized platform for users to compare product details, features, and prices, eliminating the need to visit multiple websites. The goal is to streamline decision-making, making it more efficient and user-friendly. The app aims to enhance the overall shopping experience by providing a convenient and straightforward way for users to make informed choices without the hassle of navigating through various online sources.

Information Architecture

Wireframes

During the wireframe phase, it became clear that some essential features were missing, most notably the option for users to review and confirm their orders before finalizing a purchase. Without this step, users might feel uncertain, lacking the chance to verify selections, quantities, and pricing. This confirmation screen is critical in e-commerce, providing reassurance and transparency by displaying details like delivery times, discounts, and payment methods. Recognizing this gap, we updated the wireframes to include a dedicated confirmation step, ensuring a smoother, more confident user experience from selection to checkout.

Testing

In the testing phase, I aimed to ensure the design was intuitive, functional, and aligned with user needs. I started with usability testing, where I guided users through key tasks like comparing products, browsing options, and placing an order. While feedback was positive overall, a few areas for improvement emerged. Some users, for instance, felt that the placement of the “Confirm Order” button was unclear, so I adjusted its position and styling to make it more prominent and easy to find. Additionally, stakeholder reviews provided further insights. They recommended tweaks to improve brand alignment, such a adjusting the color scheme for call-to-action buttons and refining copy to feel more engaging and approachable. After implementing these updates, I conducted a final round of testing to verify that the changes enhanced usability and visual clarity. These tweaks made a noticeable difference, as users found it easier to navigate and complete key actions confidently. Overall, the testing process was a success, revealing small but meaningful adjustments that ultimately improved the experience and ensured the design met both user expectations and stakeholder goals.

Final Product

In essence, this app strives to streamline the shopping journey, providing a centralized hub for easy product comparisons and direct in-app purchases. The goal is to empower users with the tools to make informed decisions effortlessly. Moreover, the app seeks to create a user-friendly environment, simplifying the complex process of comparing and facilitating straightforward transactions within the app. Its overarching aim is to make the entire shopping experience more efficient and user-centric.

Teachings From the Project

As my first project, this experience was an invaluable introduction to the real-world challenges and rewards of UX design. I learned the importance of balancing user needs with client expectations and saw firsthand how even small design choices can greatly impact usability. Navigating the project from wireframing to stakeholder feedback taught me the value of flexibility, as initial ideas often evolved significantly through testing and collaboration. When I realized that key features like the order confirmation step were missing, it reinforced the need for a structured, iterative process to ensure essential elements aren’t overlooked. This project strengthened my commitment to user-centered design, emphasizing that every feature should enhance the user journey. Ultimately, this experience provided a solid foundation in UX fundamentals, helping me build confidence and a deeper understanding of what it takes to create intuitive, satisfying user experiences.
